Soy Quick Pickles

Description

My hyper-fixation snack.

I almost always have a jar of these in the fridge, but they never last more than a day or two.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound japanese or persion cucumbers
  • 1 large clove garlic, grated
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tbsp vinegar, I like 1 apple cider + 1 rice wine personally
  • Pinch of sugar
  • Splash of toasted sesame oil
  • Kosher salt

Steps

  1. Thickly slice cucumbers and place in a colander. Make nice bite size pieces.
  2. Heavily salt cucumbers and toss to coat. Set aside to draw out moisture.
  3. Meanwhile mix all other ingredients in a large jar.
  4. After a lot of moisture has drawn out of the cucumbers, or as long as you can wait, drain and rinse.
  5. Place the cucumbers in the jar with your brine and tip to ensure everything is coated.
  6. Place in fridge, turning jar over periodically to ensure that all cucumbers pickle.
  7. Enjoy as a garnish on any rice or noodle bowl. Or just eat straight out of the fridge standing in the kitchen like I do.

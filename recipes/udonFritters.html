Udon Fritters

Description

Delicious fritters that bring the flavor of gyoza without the fuss of folding

The are dangerously addictive and perfect for a snack or appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound frozen udon noodles
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 2 eggs
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced scallions
  • 1 Tbsp. soy sauce
  • ¼ cup potato starch (I’ve found that cornstarch and all-purpose flour work too)
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated
  • 1 Tbsp. freshly grated ginger
  • 1 Tbsp. vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Mix all ingredients but the oil in a large bowl until combined, but not over mixed.
  2. Heat 1 Tbsp. vegetable oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Using a standard ice cream scoop or a ¼-cup measure, measure out balls of the udon gyoza mixture.
  4. Flatten each ball into a ½-inch thick patty in the palms of your hands, then place four to five patties in the hot skillet, taking care not to crowd the pan.
  5. Cover the skillet, and cook for 5 minutes, undisturbed, until deep golden brown
  6. Flip the udon fritters and cook, uncovered, for an additional 3 minutes, until cooked through.
  7. Serve with a dipping sauce made of equal parts soy sauce or ponzu and black vinegar.
